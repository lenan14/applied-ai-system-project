# Cat Care Guidelines

## Feeding

Adult cats should be fed measured portions twice per day rather than free-feeding, which contributes to obesity. Cats are obligate carnivores requiring high-protein diets. Fresh water should always be available; many cats prefer running water from a fountain. Wet food contributes to hydration and urinary tract health.

## Enrichment and Play

Cats require daily interactive play to prevent boredom and maintain physical fitness. Sessions of 10 to 15 minutes using wand toys or laser pointers simulate hunting behavior. Without enrichment, indoor cats commonly develop stress-related behaviors including over-grooming, aggression, and litter box avoidance.

## Grooming

Short-haired cats groom themselves effectively but benefit from weekly brushing to reduce hairball formation. Long-haired cats require daily brushing to prevent matting, especially around the neck and hindquarters. Dental health is a major concern; aim for tooth brushing 2 to 3 times per week or dental treats daily.

## Litter Box Maintenance

A clean litter box is critical for cat health and prevents inappropriate elimination. Scoop daily and fully replace litter weekly. Cats with urinary tract conditions require extra monitoring; unusual odor or straining to urinate requires immediate veterinary attention.

## Medication

Cats metabolize many medications differently than dogs or humans. Never give a cat medications prescribed for dogs. Oral liquid medications are often better tolerated than pills. For diabetic cats, insulin injections should be given at 12-hour intervals paired with meals.
