# Pet Medication Guidelines

## Timing and Consistency

Consistent medication timing is essential for therapeutic effectiveness. For medications with a 12-hour dosing interval, administer as close to 12 hours apart as possible each day. For 24-hour intervals, try to give the medication at the same time daily. Inconsistent timing causes drug levels in the bloodstream to fluctuate, reducing efficacy or causing adverse effects.

## Insulin for Diabetic Pets

Diabetic dogs and cats require insulin injections, typically twice daily with meals. The dose must be calibrated by a veterinarian and should not be adjusted without professional guidance. Signs of hypoglycemia (too much insulin) include weakness, disorientation, and seizures. Always have a glucose source available and contact your vet immediately if you observe these symptoms.

## Antibiotics

Complete the full antibiotic course even if your pet appears to have recovered. Stopping early causes bacterial resistance and relapse. Most antibiotics should be given with food to reduce gastrointestinal upset. Store antibiotics as directed, as some require refrigeration.

## Medications with Food Restrictions

Some medications interact with certain foods. Avoid giving dairy products within 2 hours of fluoroquinolone antibiotics, as calcium binds the drug and reduces absorption. Check with your vet or pharmacist before introducing new foods while your pet is on medication.

## Missed Doses

If you miss a dose, give it as soon as you remember unless it is almost time for the next scheduled dose. In that case, skip the missed dose and continue the regular schedule. Do not double up doses.

## Safe Storage and Disposal

Store all pet medications in a secure location away from children and other pets. Many medications are toxic if consumed by the wrong species or in the wrong amount. Dispose of unused medications at a pharmacy take-back program rather than flushing them.
