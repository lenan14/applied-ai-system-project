# Dog Care Guidelines

## Daily Exercise Requirements

Dogs need daily physical activity to maintain healthy weight, cardiovascular function, and mental stimulation. Most adult dogs require 30 to 60 minutes of exercise per day depending on breed. High-energy breeds such as Border Collies or Huskies may need 90 minutes or more. Insufficient exercise leads to destructive behavior, obesity, and anxiety.

## Feeding Schedule

Adult dogs should be fed twice daily at consistent times, typically morning and evening. Consistent meal timing regulates digestion and helps prevent bloat, a life-threatening condition in large breeds. Puppies under 6 months require three meals per day. Avoid feeding immediately before or after vigorous exercise.

## Medication Administration

Medications for dogs should be administered at the same time each day to maintain steady blood concentration levels. If your dog takes insulin for diabetes, injections must be paired with meals and given within a 30-minute window. Missing doses or inconsistent timing can cause dangerous hypoglycemia or hyperglycemia.

## Grooming

Regular grooming prevents matting, skin infections, and parasite infestations. Short-coated breeds need brushing weekly, while long-coated breeds need brushing 3 to 4 times per week. Nails should be trimmed every 3 to 4 weeks to prevent overgrowth and joint strain.

## Training and Enrichment

Mental stimulation is as important as physical exercise for dogs. Training sessions of 10 to 15 minutes reinforce positive behavior and strengthen the human-pet bond. Puzzle feeders, sniff walks, and novel environments provide cognitive enrichment that reduces anxiety and boredom.
